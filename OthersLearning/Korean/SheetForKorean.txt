11. 동사, 형용사 + -어요/-아요
ㅏ, ㅗ X 있다, 없다 있어요, 없어요
ㅏ, ㅗ O 맑다, 많다 맑아요, 많아요
하다 좋아하다, 공부하다 좋아해요, 공부해요

● 덥다,춥다의경우‘더워요,추워요’로씁니다.
덥다:덥+어요 =더워요
춥다:춥 +어요 = 추워요
● 동사,형용사가‘ㅏ’로 끝나면‘가요’처럼씁니다.
ㅏㅇ/받침X 가- + -아요 =가요
● 동사,형용사가‘ㅗ’로 끝나면‘와요’처럼씁니다.
ㅗㅇ/받침X 오- + -아요 =와요
● 동사 ,형용사가‘ㅣ’로 끝나면‘-ㅕ요’로 씁니다.
가르치- + -어요 =가르치어요 =가르쳐요
● 동사 ,형용사가‘ㅡ’로 끝나면그앞의모음을 봅니다
나쁘- + -아요 =나빠요

가다 (to go)	가	가요	I go / You go
오다 (to come)	오	와요	I come / You come
먹다 (to eat)	먹	먹어요	I eat / You eat
마시다 (to drink)	마시	마셔요 (shortened)	I drink / You drink
보다 (to see)	보	봐요	I see / You see
있다 (to exist)	있	있어요	It exists / I have
없다 (to not exist)	없	없어요	It doesn’t exist / I don’t have
하다 (to do)	하	해요	I do / You do
공부하다 (to study)	공부하	공부해요	I study / You study

Irregular Cases:
ㄷ-irregular verbs: Some verbs change ㄷ to ㄹ before adding 어요.
듣다 (to listen) → 들어요 (not 듣어요)
걷다 (to walk) → 걸어요 (but 걷다 as "to fold" stays 걷어요)
르-irregular verbs: If 르 is in the stem, add 러요 and double the consonant before it.
빠르다 (to be fast) → 빨라요
부르다 (to sing/call) → 불러요
ㅂ-irregular verbs: Some ㅂ verbs change to 워요.
춥다 (to be cold) → 추워요
쉽다 (to be easy) → 쉬워요

15. (장소)에  : It is attached after a location noun to indicate the person or the object’s existence and location.
가: 휴대 전화가 어디에 있어요?
나: 휴대 전화가 책상 위에 있어요.

16. 에서 / 나: 방에서 쉬어요 
It is attached after a location noun to indicate where the action is happening.
It can be used with various action verbs
 
17. -었-  : It is attached after a verb, an adjective, ‘이다’, or ‘아니다’ to indicate that the situation or the
action has taken place in the past  / 날씨가 좋았어요

먹었다, 썼다
갔다, 만났다
공부했다, 운동했다

18. (시간)에 It is attached after a noun to indicate when the action or the state has taken/will take place.

19. 부터 : It is attached after a noun to indicate when the action or the state has started/will start.
명사 + 부터 Noun+ 부터

20. -지 못하다 : It is attached after a verb to deny the situation or show inability for the action.
동사 + -지 못하다 Verb+ -지 못하다

먹지 못하다, 만들지 못하다
못 먹다, 못 만들다

21. -을까 : It is attached after a verb to ask for others’ opinion on doing the action.

동사 + -을까요/-ㄹ까요 Verb+ -을까요/-ㄹ까요 

먹을까요
갈까요

22. -읍시다 : It is attached after a verb to suggest others to do the action together.
It is used in formal situations, but not used towards the elderly or superiors.
Can be used as an answer to ‘-을까요?
동사 + -읍시다/-ㅂ시다 Verb+ -읍시다/-ㅂ시다

23. -고 :  It is attached after a verb or an adjective to list actions or states.
나: 저는 밥을 먹고 동생은 우유를 마셔요.

24. -어서 : it is attached after a verb or an adjective to indicate that the context before it is the
reason or the cause of the context after it.

먹어서

나: 배가 고파서 식당에 가요.

25. -고 싶다 : It is attached after a verb to show desire for the action of the speaker

가: 쯔엉 씨, 방학 때 뭐 하고 싶어요?
나: 한정식 맛집에 가고 싶어요.

26. -고 싶다 :  It is attached after a verb to show desire for the action of the speaker.

가: 쯔엉 씨, 방학 때 뭐 하고 싶어요?
나: 한정식 맛집에 가고 싶어요.
